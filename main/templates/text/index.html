Findings

  • This study reveals some concerning patterns emerging around crime around the Westminster area.
  • On top of its exceptionally high rates of theft related crimes, it also scores highly in other more serious categories such as violent crime.
  • Generally, the safest areas of London are in the South-West of the city in boroughs such as Richmond upon Thames and Kingston upon Thames.

Next Steps and Caveats

  • It must also be acknowledged that Westminster may experience such high rates of theft-related crime because it sees increased tourist footfall: Westminster houses some of London's most iconic landmarks.
  • An intriguing next step may be to plot the daily footfall across boroughs, to potentially investigate whether the increased levels of crime are associated with how many visitors Westminster sees.
  • My initial measurement of population density may not be as reliable since this includes residents but not overall footfall in the area.
  • However, regardless of potential causes for the crime hotspot, one thing remains clear: Westminster is the least safe borough in London.
  • Despite pledged and overall increases in London police force strength, it remains abundantly clear that Westminster requires additional police resources.

How this was produced

  • All source data, apart from borough population density (which is linked above), can be sourced here.
  • The plotly maps were produced via the following methodology:
  • 1. Download the borough boundary files from the London datastore as a .gpkg file. Convert to a GEOJSON file using EPSG:4326.
  • 2. Plot a choropleth map and overlay the borough level data from the London datastore. A code snippet is shown below.
                  
totalcrimes = pd.read_csv(totalcrimescsv_path)
fig2 = px.choropleth(
        totalcrimes,
        locations="Borough",
        geojson=ldnboroughs,
        color="Criminal Offences 2023-25",
        hover_name="Borough",
        hover_data=["Criminal Offences 2023-25"],
        title="Total Crime 2023-25",
    )
fig2.update_geos(fitbounds="locations", visible=False)
fig2.update_layout(margin={"r":0,"t":0,"l":0,"b":0})
plot_div2 = fig2.to_html(full_html=False, include_plotlyjs=False)
